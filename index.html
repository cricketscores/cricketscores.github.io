1. ICC Cricket World Cup 2023 Venue: Wankhede Stadium, Mumbai

The ICC Cricket World Cup 2023 will take place at the iconic Wankhede Stadium in Mumbai, India. This stadium can accommodate over 33,000 spectators and is renowned for its modern facilities and comfortable seating. Over the years, this venue has hosted many major cricket tournaments, including the 2011 Cricket World Cup final. This stadium will be a gathering place for cricket players and fans from around the world, bringing together elite players and cricket enthusiasts to celebrate the excitement and charm of this remarkable sport.

2. ICC Cricket World Cup 2023 Venue: M.A. Chidambaram Stadium, Chennai

The MA Chidambaram Stadium in Chennai, India is a renowned cricket stadium, home to the Tamil Nadu cricket team. It has a seating capacity of 38,000 and has been host to many high-profile cricket matches, including the Indian Premier League and the Cricket World Cup. The stadium is named after the former President of the Board of Control for Cricket in India, M.A. Chidambaram. It is also known as the "Chepauk Stadium" due to its location in the Chepauk neighborhood of Chennai. With its rich history and modern facilities, the MA Chidambaram Stadium is one of the most sought-after venues for international cricket matches and a hub of cricketing excellence.

3. ICC Cricket World Cup 2023 Venue: Sawai Mansingh Stadium, Jaipur

Sawai Mansingh Stadium, located in Jaipur, India, is a cricket stadium that boasts a seating capacity of over 30,000. It is home to the Rajasthan Royals, a team that competes in the Indian Premier League. The stadium was named after former Maharaja of Jaipur, Sawai Mansingh II, who contributed significantly to the development of sports in the region. The stadium is equipped with modern facilities and has hosted several international cricket matches, including the 1987 and 1996 Cricket World Cup. Sawai Mansingh Stadium is a prominent cricketing venue, known for its electrifying atmosphere and fantastic sporting performances.

4. ICC Cricket World Cup 2023 Venue: Sheikh Zayed Stadium, Abu Dhabi

The Sheikh Zayed Stadium, located in Abu Dhabi, is one of the most famous cricket grounds in the Middle East. The stadium has a seating capacity of 20,000 and is equipped with excellent facilities, including floodlights for day-night matches. It is home to the Abu Dhabi cricket team and many international tournaments are held here, including the Indian Premier League, the Pakistan Super League and the T10 League. The Stadium was named in honor of Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan, the founder of the United Arab Emirates. It is a hub for cricket enthusiasts in the Middle East, offering a unique experience of watching cricket in the heart of the desert.

5. ICC Cricket World Cup 2023 Venue: Eden Gardens Stadium, Kolkata

Eden Gardens Stadium, located in Kolkata, India, is one of the oldest and most iconic cricket stadiums in the world. It has a seating capacity of over 66,000 spectators, making it the largest cricket stadium in India. The stadium has hosted many high-profile cricket matches, including the 1987 and 1996 Cricket World Cup finals. It is also the home ground of the Kolkata Knight Riders, a team in the Indian Premier League. The stadium has a rich history and is one of the most important cricketing venues in India, known for its electrifying atmosphere and passionate cricket fans.

6. ICC Cricket World Cup 2023 Venue: Motera Stadium, Ahmedabad

Motera Stadium, located in Ahmedabad, India, is a cricket stadium with a seating capacity of over 110,000, making it the largest cricket stadium in the world. The stadium is a state-of-the-art facility that has recently undergone a major renovation, featuring world-class amenities like a state-of-the-art gymnasium, Olympic size swimming pool, and 76 corporate boxes. The stadium hosted its first major test match in February 2021, between India and England, where a limited number of spectators were allowed due to COVID-19 restrictions. Motera Stadium is poised to become a hub of cricketing excellence, a venue that can host major international tournaments and offer a world-class experience to cricket fans.

7. ICC Cricket World Cup 2023 Venue: Arun Jaitley Stadium, Delhi

The Arun Jaitley Stadium, formerly known as the Feroz Shah Kotla Stadium, is a cricket stadium located in Delhi, India. It has a seating capacity of over 41,000 spectators and is home to the Delhi cricket team and the Delhi Capitals team in the Indian Premier League. The stadium has hosted many major international cricket matches, including the 2011 Cricket World Cup. It was renamed in honor of Arun Jaitley, a prominent Indian politician and former president of the Delhi and District Cricket Association. The Arun Jaitley Stadium is a landmark of Indian cricketing heritage, known for its passionate cricket fans and memorable sporting performances.
